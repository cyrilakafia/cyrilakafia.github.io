Bio

I am an incoming Ph.D. student in the UNITES Lab at University of North Carolina, Chapel Hill, advised by Prof. Tianlong Chen. Prior to UNC, I obtained an M.S. in ISysE at KAIST, where I was fortunate to be advised by Prof. Chanyoung Park. During my Master's, I was honored with the Best Paper Award at the ICML WCB 2023 and spent wonderful time in Tokyo as a visiting researcher at Tokyo Tech, working with Prof. Tsuyoshi Murata and, Dr. Xin Liu.

My current research intersts ile in AI4Science and Efficient ML. Specifically, I am interested in (A) addressing the inductive bias of Graph Neural Networks (GNNs) and Large Language Models (LLMs) with their (B) application in the field of Computational Biology, particularly single-cell RNA sequencing and Tumor Biology. Additionally, I have recently been focusing on (C) building efficient Sparse Mixture-of-Experts in diverse scientific fields.
