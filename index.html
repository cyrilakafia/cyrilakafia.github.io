Bio

I study how the brain encodes information at the population level, and how we can build closed-loop systems that use that structure to modulate neural circuits. I am currently a postgraduate research associate in the AZA Lab at Yale University, advised by AZA Allsop, MD, PhD.

I will join the Chen Ultrasound Lab at Washington University in St. Louis as a PhD student in Biomedical Engineering, where my work will focus on closed-loop neuromodulation using focused ultrasound.

I grew up in Ghana and completed my B.Sc. in Biomedical Engineering at the University of Ghana, where I was advised by Prof. Samuel K. Kwofie. I organize NeuroAI workshops at the Deep Learning Indaba, which has brought together 150+ researchers across Africa, and I am committed to mentorship for students from underrepresented backgrounds in STEM.

Upcoming

Closed-Loop Focused Ultrasound

Can we use the brain's own activity to guide when and where to stimulate? My upcoming PhD work in the Chen Ultrasound Lab will develop closed-loop systems that use real-time neural recordings to drive focused ultrasound stimulation, enabling non-invasive, circuit-specific neuromodulation without surgery.

Visit lab →

Functional Encoding Units in Neural Ensembles

Large-scale neural recordings contain rich population-level structure that single-neuron analyses miss. We developed FEU, an unsupervised method to partition neuronal responses into functional ensembles based on shared activity patterns. Applied to social behavior data, FEU reveals interpretable attractor-like dynamics that track behavioral state.

Read preprint →

Music Mindfulness for Mental Health

We examined whether a brief structured music mindfulness session could produce measurable physiological and psychological changes in adults with anxiety and depression. Using HRV and self-report measures, we found acute improvements in autonomic tone and mood, supporting music as a low-barrier adjunct mental health intervention.

Read paper →

Biomedical AI for Discovery and Diagnosis

Early work applying machine learning to biomedical discovery problems, including AICpred, a model that screens small molecules for anti-inflammatory activity by predicting TLR4-MyD88 binding disruption, and clinical imaging models to support radiology workflows in low-resource settings.
