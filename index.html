Bio

I am a postgraduate research assistant working in the Ph.D. student in the AZA Lab at Yale University, advised by AZA Allsop, MD, PhD. Prior to my current research position, I obtained a B.Sc in Engineering (Biomedical) at the University of Ghana, where I was fortunate to be advised by Prof. Samuel K Kwofie.

My passion lies in the fields of Computational Biology, Computation Neuroscience and Artificial Intelligence. My research interest is in understanding and decoding the both the latent and otherwise more explicit neural representations of cognitive processes that drive behavior. Additionally, I am interested in motor control and speech, and how these processes are altered in disease states. My research at the AZA Lab is primarily focused on developing and using tools for the analysis of non-linear time series data. I also focus on decoding electrophysiological data from social and behavioral paradigms to understand relationships between actions, their stimuli and their corresponding neural representations. Concurrently, I explore the effects of connectedness and mindfulness on neural representations and overall mental health.
